Thesis Statement
Broadcom is the structurally dominant supplier of custom AI accelerators (XPUs) and AI networking silicon to the world's largest AI platform builders. Paired with a sticky, high-margin infrastructure software franchise anchored by VMware, the company is transforming into a two-engine compounder with some of the most durable forward growth visibility in large-cap technology. At $350.63 — 15.4% below its 52-week high — the market is offering a 27.2x FY2026E P/E and 15.7x FY2027E P/E on a business guiding for >$100B in AI chip revenue by fiscal 2027.

AI Infrastructure Super-Cycle — The Structural Tailwind
The defining macro backdrop for AVGO is the accelerating shift of hyperscaler capital expenditure toward AI compute infrastructure. Google, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, and OpenAI collectively guided to over $300B in AI infrastructure spend in 2025, with 2026–2027 guidance pointing higher. This spend falls into two categories that AVGO dominates: custom AI accelerators (XPUs) for model training and inference, and high-speed Ethernet switching (Tomahawk family) for connecting GPU/XPU clusters at scale.
Unlike commodity hardware cycles, AI infrastructure buildout is driven by competitive necessity — no hyperscaler can afford to fall behind on AI compute capacity. This creates multi-year committed demand with locked supply agreements, insulating AVGO from the demand volatility typical of semiconductor cycles. Management has locked capacity agreements through 2028 across wafers, HBM, and substrates.
Business Overview
Broadcom Inc. (NASDAQ: AVGO) is a global technology infrastructure company operating two high-margin segments under CEO Hock Tan, who has compounded revenue at 23.5% annually since FY2021 through organic growth and serial, disciplined M&A.

Competitive Moat
  • 20+ Years ASIC Design Experience — Broadcom has designed custom silicon for hyperscalers for over two decades. The manufacturing yield, SerDes IP, and co-design expertise are near-impossible to replicate quickly.
  • Locked Supply Agreements — Capacity for TSMC N3/N2, HBM, and advanced packaging committed through 2028. Competitors face 2–3 year lead times to replicate.
  • Tomahawk Networking Monopoly — Tomahawk 6 (100 Tbps) had no announced equal at launch. Next-gen Tomahawk 7 planned for 2027 maintains the lead.
  • VMware Network Effect — VCF is the abstraction layer for enterprise AI compute. Switching costs are extremely high — customers who standardize on VCF do not leave.
  • Hock Tan's M&A Track Record — Broadcom, CA Technologies, Symantec, and VMware all acquired at defensible prices and integrated to expand FCF margins.

Primary Risks
  • High
    AI Revenue Execution Gap
    The FY2027 >$100B AI chip target requires growing from a ~$43B annualized Q2 run rate to $100B+ — a 2.5x step-up in 12 months. If Anthropic, Meta, or OpenAI ramps slip 6–12 months, consensus estimates collapse and the stock de-rates sharply.
  • High
    Customer Concentration
    Google and Meta together likely represent 50%+ of XPU revenue. A design-out decision (switching to competing ASIC vendor or in-house chip) by either customer would be an existential revenue event. No public disclosure of customer-specific revenue concentration.
  • Medium
    Customer-Owned Tooling (COT) Risk
    Amazon (Trainium 3), Microsoft (Maia 2), Meta (internal ASIC team) all investing in internal chip design. This is a 3–5 year risk, not 2026, but could limit TAM growth if hyperscalers choose to bring design in-house after current contracts expire.
  • Medium
    Hyperscaler CapEx Moderation
    If AI ROI disappointment causes hyperscalers to reduce 2027 CapEx guidance (as seen briefly in early 2025), AVGO's revenue ramp is directly impacted. AI semiconductor spend is the most cyclical part of the business despite "committed" guidance.
Secondary Risks
  • Medium
    VMware Churn / Enterprise Spending
    VMware's subscription transition creates a short-term ARR recognition headwind. If enterprises churn off VCF onto alternative hypervisor solutions (Nutanix, open-source KVM), the $27B software segment growth thesis weakens.
  • Medium
    Balance Sheet Leverage
    $65B gross debt ($49B net) leaves limited flexibility for another large acquisition until FCF deleverages the balance sheet. Net Debt/EBITDA of 1.14x is manageable but limits optionality.
  • Lower
    Tariff & Supply Chain
    Fabless model limits direct tariff exposure but TSMC capacity in Taiwan creates geopolitical concentration. TSMC is building Arizona fabs (N4/N3) but advanced N2 capacity remains Taiwan-centric through 2027.
  • Lower
    Goodwill Impairment
    $130.1B of goodwill + intangibles on the balance sheet. Any VMware integration failure or enterprise software revenue deterioration could trigger a non-cash impairment. Does not affect FCF but compresses book value and triggers negative headlines.
